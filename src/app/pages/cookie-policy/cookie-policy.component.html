1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners. At NAPLAN Bridge, we use cookies to enhance your learning experience and improve our platform's functionality.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the platform to function properly. They enable core features such as:

  • User authentication and login sessions
  • Security and fraud prevention
  • Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Shopping cart functionality for course purchases

Note: Essential cookies cannot be disabled as they are critical for platform operation.

2.2 Performance Cookies

These cookies help us understand how students interact with our platform:

  • Tracking page views and navigation paths
  • Measuring practice test completion rates
  • Identifying technical issues and errors
  • Analyzing platform performance and loading times

2.3 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization:

  • Remembering your year level and subject preferences
  • Saving progress in practice tests and lessons
  • Customizing content recommendations based on your learning history
  • Storing accessibility preferences (font size, contrast settings)

2.4 Analytics Cookies

We use analytics tools to improve our educational content:

  • Google Analytics (anonymized data)
  • Learning pattern analysis
  • Content effectiveness measurement
  • User engagement metrics

2.5 Marketing Cookies

With your consent, we may use cookies for:

  • Showing relevant educational content recommendations
  • Measuring the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns
  • Providing personalized course suggestions

Your Control: You can opt-out of marketing cookies at any time through your browser or account settings.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies may be set by third-party services we use:

  • Google Analytics: For website traffic analysis
  • Payment Processors: For secure transaction handling
  • Video Hosting: For embedded educational videos

These third parties have their own cookie policies, which we encourage you to review.

5. How to Manage Cookies

5.1 Browser Settings

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies
  • Mozilla Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies

5.2 Cookie Consent Manager

When you first visit NAPLAN Bridge, you'll see a cookie consent banner. You can:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Customize your cookie preferences
  • Change your preferences at any time through account settings

Important: Blocking some cookies may affect your ability to use certain features of the platform.

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. Currently, there is no industry consensus on how to respond to DNT signals. We may not alter our data collection practices when we receive DNT signals from your browser.

7. Mobile Devices and Apps

If you access NAPLAN Bridge through a mobile device or app, we may use similar technologies (such as device identifiers and local storage) to provide functionality and collect data. You can manage these through your device settings.

8. Children's Privacy and Cookies

We are committed to protecting children's privacy. For users under 18:

  • We use cookies only for essential educational functions and progress tracking
  • We do not use cookies for behavioral advertising to children
  • Parents can review and manage cookie settings on behalf of their children
  • We comply with all applicable Australian laws regarding children's data

9. Changes to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology or legal requirements. We will notify you of significant changes through our platform or via email.
